Should I Repair or Replace My Heating System?

One of the most common questions homeowners face is whether to repair their current heating system or invest in a new one. The answer depends on several factors, including the age of your equipment, the frequency of repairs, and your energy costs. Here are some guidelines to help you decide.

Consider Repair If

  • Your system is less than 10 years old
  • The issue is minor and isolated, such as a faulty thermostat or a clogged filter
  • The cost of the repair is significantly less than the cost of replacement
  • Your system has been well-maintained and has no history of recurring problems
  • Your energy bills have remained steady over the past few years

Consider Replacement If

  • Your system is 15 or more years old
  • You are calling for repairs frequently, especially within the same season
  • Your energy bills have been steadily rising despite regular maintenance
  • Some rooms in your home are consistently too hot or too cold
  • Your system uses an outdated or discontinued refrigerant

Not sure which option is right for you? Contact us for a free consultation — we will give you our honest recommendation based on what makes the most sense for your home and budget.
